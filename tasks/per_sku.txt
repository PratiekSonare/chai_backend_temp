Here’s a comprehensive set of **SKU-wise performance metrics** commonly used in retail, e-commerce, inventory management, and supply chain operations. These help evaluate how individual Stock Keeping Units (SKUs) contribute to sales, profitability, efficiency, and overall business health.

Metrics are grouped into categories for clarity, with definitions, formulas (where applicable), and why they matter at the SKU level.

### 1. Sales Performance Metrics
These measure revenue generation and demand for each SKU.

- **Units Sold (Sales Volume)**: Total number of units sold for the SKU in a period (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly).  
  *Why it matters*: Identifies high- vs. low-demand items and tracks velocity.

- **Revenue Contribution**: Total sales revenue generated by the SKU.  
  Often expressed as **Revenue % Contribution** = (SKU Revenue / Total Revenue) × 100.  
  *Why it matters*: Shows which SKUs drive the majority of top-line revenue.

- **Unit Sales % Contribution**: (SKU Units Sold / Total Units Sold) × 100.  
  *Why it matters*: Highlights volume leaders independent of pricing.

- **Average Units Sold per Day/Week**: Units sold ÷ Number of days/weeks in the period.  
  *Why it matters*: Useful for demand forecasting and reorder planning.

- **Percentage Change in Sales**: ((Current Period Units/Revenue – Prior Period) / Prior Period) × 100.  
  *Why it matters*: Detects growth, decline, or seasonality for each SKU.

### 2. Inventory Efficiency & Turnover Metrics
These assess how well inventory for the SKU is managed.

- **Inventory Turnover Rate** (SKU-level): Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) for the SKU ÷ Average Inventory Value for the SKU.  
  *Why it matters*: High turnover = efficient sales and low holding costs; low turnover signals overstocking or weak demand.

- **Days Sales of Inventory (DSI) / Days on Hand / Days Inventory Outstanding**: (Average Inventory Value ÷ COGS) × 365 (or 30/7 for shorter periods).  
  *Why it matters*: Indicates how many days the current stock will last at current sales pace.

- **Weeks on Hand**: Similar to DSI but using weeks (Average Inventory ÷ Weekly COGS).  
  *Why it matters*: Common in retail for replenishment decisions.

- **Stock-to-Sales Ratio**: Average Inventory Value ÷ Net Sales Value.  
  *Why it matters*: Shows if inventory levels align with actual sales; ideal ratios vary by industry.

- **Sell-Through Rate**: (Units Sold ÷ Units Received/Initial Stock) × 100 (often over 30/60/90 days).  
  *Why it matters*: Measures how quickly new stock sells; low rates (<40% in 90 days) may indicate poor buying or pricing issues.

### 3. Profitability Metrics
These evaluate financial contribution beyond just sales volume.

- **Gross Margin per SKU**: (Revenue – COGS) for the SKU.  
  Often as **Gross Margin %** = (Gross Margin ÷ Revenue) × 100.

- **Gross Margin Return on Inventory Investment (GMROI)**: Gross Margin ÷ Average Inventory Cost.  
  *Why it matters*: Measures profit earned per dollar invested in inventory for that SKU. Higher is better.

- **Net Profit / SKU Profitability**: Revenue – (COGS + Holding Costs + Fulfillment Costs + Other Variable Costs).  
  *Why it matters*: Reveals true bottom-line contributors after all direct costs.

- **Revenue per Unit**: Total Revenue ÷ Units Sold.  
  *Why it matters*: Tracks pricing effectiveness or average selling price (including promotions).

### 4. Operational & Risk Metrics
These highlight service levels and potential issues.

- **Stockout Rate / Lost Sales Ratio**: Number of days out of stock ÷ Total days in period (or estimated lost units).  
  *Why it matters*: Indicates missed opportunities due to poor availability.

- **Backorder Rate**: Number of backordered units ÷ Total demand.  
  *Why it matters*: Signals supply chain or forecasting problems.

- **Return Rate**: (Returned Units ÷ Units Sold) × 100.  
  *Why it matters*: High returns may point to quality, sizing, or description issues for the SKU.

- **On-Shelf Availability (OSA)**: Percentage of time the SKU is in stock and available on the shelf/floor.  
  *Why it matters*: Critical for brick-and-mortar or omnichannel retail.

### 5. Advanced / Composite Metrics
- **ABC Classification**: Categorize SKUs as A (high revenue/volume, e.g., top 20%), B (medium), C (low) based on Pareto analysis (80/20 rule).  
  *Why it matters*: Prioritizes management efforts (tight control on A items).

- **Contribution to Total Profit**: SKU’s share of overall gross or net profit.

- **Holding Cost per SKU**: (Storage + Insurance + Obsolescence + Capital Cost) per unit or period.  
  *Why it matters*: Helps calculate true cost of slow-moving items.

### How to Use These Metrics Effectively
- **Dashboarding**: Track them in tools like Excel, Tableau, Power BI, or inventory software (e.g., by SKU, category, channel, or location).
- **Benchmarking**: Compare against industry averages (e.g., grocery turnover ~14+, fashion lower) or internal targets.
- **Actionable Insights**:
  - High turnover + high margin → Protect and promote (star SKUs).
  - Low turnover + low margin → Consider discontinuation, markdowns, or bundling.
  - High volume but low margin → Review pricing or costs.
- **Frequency**: Daily/weekly for fast-moving goods; monthly for others.
- **Segmentation**: Analyze by channel (online vs. store), season, or region for deeper insights.

Start with the core set: **Units Sold**, **Revenue Contribution**, **Inventory Turnover**, **Sell-Through Rate**, and **GMROI**—these cover most decision-making needs for SKU rationalization and optimization.
